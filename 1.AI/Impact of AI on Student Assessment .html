AI in Higher Education: Impact of AI on Student Assessment

May 4, 2023

No items found.

What a sheer volume of student work professors must assess every semester! How many papers, exams, and presentations are there to evaluate? How much does the result depend on human factors such as fatigue, stress, or a bad mood? And what about the abstracts written on exams? One thing is clear - both teachers and students could benefit from a more efficient and unbiased assessment process.

We can’t help but think about how artificial intelligence (AI) can make things better. With advancements in natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning, AI has the potential to revolutionize the way student work is assessed in higher education. This post will explore the benefits, challenges, and future possibilities of AI-driven assessment.

Consider the benefits of AI-driven assessment for professors and students

  • Time savings and reduced workload for professors. AI models can handle the initial evaluation of student work, allowing professors to focus on providing more in-depth, personalized feedback.
  • Minimized biases. AI-driven assessment ensures that students are evaluated solely based on the quality of their work, fostering a more equitable learning environment.
  • Personalized feedback for students. AI systems can identify individual strengths and weaknesses, tailoring feedback to address specific areas for improvement.
  • Fostering a growth mindset. More accurate, unbiased, and constructive feedback can lead to more effective learning, better motivation, and a growth mindset among students.

Of course, the idea to use AI in assessment does not belong to us. Institutions around the world have long begun to probe the potential of AI for evaluating student work

A notable example is Turnitin, a widely-used plagiarism detection tool. It relies on AI and machine learning to identify similarities between a submitted document and a vast database of academic papers, articles, and other content. Turnitin helps instructors detect plagiarism and maintain academic integrity in student work. While Turnitin primarily focuses on plagiarism detection, it also provides features like "Turnitin Feedback Studio" that allow instructors to leave feedback on student work and assess their writing skills.

Website: https://www.turnitin.com/ 

Then, Gradescope is an AI-driven grading platform that allows instructors to streamline the assessment process for various types of assignments, including written work, multiple-choice questions, and programming assignments. Using AI, Gradescope can group similar answers together, allowing instructors to provide feedback more efficiently. For programming assignments, Gradescope uses an autograder system that automatically assesses students' code based on predefined test cases and criteria.

Website: https://www.gradescope.com/ 

Luckily, many more applications of AI in higher education for student assessment are yet to be explored! Here’s just a few

Grading for written assignments

Educational institutions can implement an AI-driven grading system for written assignments, which can demonstrate impressive accuracy and provide personalized feedback for students. This is achieved thanks to natural language processing (NLP) models that help analyze essay language, structure, coherence, and the quality of arguments. Advanced language models like ChatGPT offer great promise in enhancing the assessment process for written assignments, bringing efficiency and effectiveness to the grading process in educational institutions.

Assessment of oral presentations

Colleges and universities can integrate AI algorithms into the assessment of oral presentations, resulting in objective scores and suggestions for improvement that are appreciated by both professors and students. The AI system can evaluate students' presentations based on criteria such as clarity, organization, and content. By using speech recognition technology and machine learning, the system can analyze verbal and nonverbal cues like tone, pace, and body language, providing valuable feedback on presentation skills.

AI-driven peer review

Implementing AI algorithms to facilitate and streamline the peer review process can help students learn from one another while improving the quality of their work. The AI system can intelligently match students for peer review based on their strengths, weaknesses, or interests, and provide guidance on offering constructive feedback. Additionally, the AI can monitor the quality of the feedback provided, ensuring that it is relevant, specific, and actionable. By guiding students through the peer review process and identifying areas where they can improve their feedback, AI-driven peer review systems can help students develop critical thinking, communication, and collaboration skills that are essential for success in higher education and beyond.

Creativity assessment

It's funny to think about assessing human creativity with artificial intelligence, isn’t it? Yet, AI can be utilized to evaluate creative works, such as art, music, or design projects, by employing advanced algorithms that analyze various elements of the work. For visual art, the AI system can analyze patterns, colors, shapes, and other elements to provide objective feedback on the originality and aesthetic quality of the artwork. In the case of music, the AI can assess melody, harmony, rhythm, and instrumentation to determine the creativity and overall quality of the composition. For design projects, AI systems can evaluate the effectiveness of the design, its visual appeal, and the use of design principles. By providing tailored feedback based on the specific creative domain, AI-powered assessment systems can help students enhance their skills in these areas and encourage their creative growth.

Still, despite the promise of AI-aided assessment, concerns and limitations exist

The task of educational institutions — and ours as an AI development company — is to address the key issues when implementing AI in the evaluation of student work.

Privacy and ethical concerns

Institutions must ensure that AI systems handle student data securely and transparently. Data protection measures must be in place to safeguard sensitive information. Additionally, addressing potential algorithmic biases is essential to ensure fair assessment for all students. This may involve conducting regular audits of AI systems to identify and rectify any biases that may emerge during the development and deployment stages.

Accuracy and reliability

The quality of AI-based assessments depends on the model's complexity and training data quality. Ongoing research and development are necessary to improve the performance of AI-driven assessment tools. The system used should encompass diverse essay topics, structures, and writing styles to enhance the accuracy and reliability of the assessment results.

Balancing human and AI input

While AI-driven assessment offers numerous benefits, it should complement, not replace, human judgment. Professors should remain involved in the assessment process to ensure a holistic evaluation of student work. This may involve using AI-generated feedback as a starting point for their review and then incorporating their expertise and contextual understanding to provide students with comprehensive and meaningful feedback.

A world where assessment and artificial intelligence go hand in hand

The future of AI-driven assessment is full of potential improvements and refinements that could revolutionize the way we evaluate student work. Let's take a look at some of the thrilling possibilities on the horizon.

Enhanced AI techniques

With AI and machine learning continuously evolving, can we expect more sophisticated algorithms to take assessment accuracy and reliability to new heights? As technology advances, there's no doubt that we'll see groundbreaking improvements in the way AI evaluates student work, making it even more precise and effective.

Expansion across disciplines

How far-reaching could the impact of AI-driven assessment be? As this technology matures, it could be applied to a wider range of subjects and educational settings, making it more inclusive and versatile. From humanities to sciences, AI-driven assessment has the potential to transform every corner of academia.

Integration with other educational technologies

What if we could combine AI-driven assessment with other cutting-edge tools, such as learning management systems and adaptive learning platforms? Integrating these technologies could create a more comprehensive and personalized educational experience for students, propelling learning to new heights.
